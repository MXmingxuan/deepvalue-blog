# Voice and tone

## Editorial stance

Deep Value writes as a careful researcher, not a promoter. The voice is factual, analytical, patient, and explicit about what remains unverified. It distinguishes evidence from inference and gives readers the conditions that could strengthen or invalidate a view.

## Language rules

- Lead with the judgment, then show the supporting facts and unresolved questions.
- Prefer concrete nouns and active verbs. Avoid slogans, urgency theater, and inflated certainty.
- Use sentence case for Chinese headings. Use full-width Chinese punctuation in Chinese prose.
- Keep English interface labels short, uppercase, and structural: `FEATURED RESEARCH`, `RESEARCH LOG`, `ARCHIVE`.
- Do not use emoji.
- Do not call work “exclusive,” “must-read,” “winning,” or “alpha.”
- Never invent prices, returns, rankings, article counts, publication issue numbers, or “live” market data.

## Facts, numbers, and metadata

- Preserve published titles, dates, company names, tickers, and units exactly.
- Use Arabic numerals for measured values and dates where they improve scanning.
- Set dates, tickers, commodity codes, confidence levels, and concise metadata in `--font-data`.
- Label estimates and inferences. State the source boundary when a number is derived rather than disclosed directly.
- When there is no published content in a section, say so plainly; do not fill the visual system with sample research.

## Publication voice

Use this approved positioning when the publication introduces itself:

> Deep Value 以投资、交易与大宗商品研究为核心，同时记录 AI 应用、研究工作流与边界之外的探索。

Calls to action are direct and informational: `阅读全文`, `进入投资研究`, `查看研究日志`, `浏览档案`. Avoid conversion language.

## Tone by context

- **Homepage:** assured and concise; establish scope without claiming coverage that has not been published.
- **Research article:** evidence-first, patient, explicit about uncertainty and verification conditions.
- **Research log:** immediate but still sourced; preserve timestamp and evolving judgment.
- **Empty state:** honest and neutral; explain what will appear without implying a publishing schedule.
- **About:** specific about scope and method, modest about authority.
